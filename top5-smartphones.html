Top 5 Phones Under $300 in 2025

Budget smartphones that deliver flagship-like performance without breaking the bank.

Budget Phones with Premium Features

The smartphone market has evolved dramatically, with budget devices now offering features that were exclusive to flagships just a few years ago. In 2025, you can get exceptional performance, impressive cameras, and long battery life without spending a fortune. Here are our top picks for phones under $300.

1

Redmi Note 14 Pro

$279

MediaTek Dimensity 7300
8GB RAM / 128GB Storage
6.67" 120Hz AMOLED
64MP Main + 8MP Ultra-wide
5,000mAh, 67W Charging
5G, Wi-Fi 6, Bluetooth 5.3

The Redmi Note 14 Pro tops our list with its exceptional balance of performance, display quality, and battery life. The 120Hz AMOLED display is vibrant and smooth, while the Dimensity 7300 processor handles everything from gaming to multitasking with ease.

Pros

  • Excellent display quality
  • Strong performance for gaming
  • Fast charging capability
  • Premium glass design

Cons

  • Average ultra-wide camera
  • No wireless charging
  • MIUI software can be bloated
2

Samsung Galaxy A55

$299

Exynos 1480
6GB RAM / 128GB Storage
6.5" 90Hz Super AMOLED
50MP Main + 12MP Ultra-wide
5,000mAh, 25W Charging
5G, Wi-Fi 6, Bluetooth 5.3

Samsung's Galaxy A55 offers the most refined software experience in this price range, with promised updates for 4 years. The camera system is versatile and reliable, producing excellent photos in most lighting conditions.

Pros

  • Clean One UI software
  • Long software support
  • IP67 water/dust resistance
  • Excellent build quality

Cons

  • Slower charging than competitors
  • Occasional performance hiccups
  • No charger in box
3

Nothing Phone (2a)

$249

MediaTek Dimensity 7200
8GB RAM / 128GB Storage
6.7" 120Hz OLED
50MP Main + 50MP Ultra-wide
4,700mAh, 45W Charging
5G, Wi-Fi 6, Bluetooth 5.3

The Nothing Phone (2a) stands out with its unique transparent design and Glyph interface. Beyond aesthetics, it delivers a clean, near-stock Android experience with minimal bloatware and smooth performance.

Pros

  • Distinctive design
  • Clean, bloat-free software
  • Balanced camera system
  • Smooth 120Hz display

Cons

  • No expandable storage
  • Average battery life
  • Limited availability in some regions
4

Motorola Moto G85

$269

Snapdragon 6 Gen 3
6GB RAM / 128GB Storage
6.6" 120Hz pOLED
50MP Main + 8MP Ultra-wide
5,000mAh, 30W Charging
5G, Wi-Fi 6, Bluetooth 5.3

Motorola continues its tradition of offering excellent value with the Moto G85. The near-stock Android experience is complemented by thoughtful Moto enhancements, and the battery life is exceptional, easily lasting through a full day of heavy use.

Pros

  • Clean software experience
  • Excellent battery life
  • Expandable storage
  • 3.5mm headphone jack

Cons

  • Average camera performance
  • Plastic build feels less premium
  • Limited software updates
5

Poco F5

$299

Snapdragon 7+ Gen 2
8GB RAM / 256GB Storage
6.67" 120Hz AMOLED
64MP Main + 8MP Ultra-wide
5,000mAh, 67W Charging
5G, Wi-Fi 6, Bluetooth 5.3

The Poco F5 offers the best raw performance in this price range, with a processor that can compete with much more expensive phones. It's the ideal choice for gamers and power users who want maximum performance without breaking the bank.

Pros

  • Flagship-level performance
  • Excellent display
  • Fast charging
  • Good value for money

Cons

  • MIUI software is feature-heavy
  • Camera inconsistent in low light
  • No wireless charging

The Verdict

Budget smartphones have come a long way, and in 2025, you don't need to spend a fortune to get a great mobile experience. Each of these phones offers exceptional value with different strengths. The Redmi Note 14 Pro offers the best all-around package, but depending on your priorities—whether it's software experience, camera quality, or raw performance—any of these options would serve you well.
